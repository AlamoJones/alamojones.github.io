Experiences with intent, enviroment

Background

I have a real personal problem with consumption and executive function. Some level of this runs in the family - my grandfather seemed to have been a good man when I met him (albiet when he was 80ish, and I was 15ish), but of his 3 sons, 1 died a drunkard and another has in and out of jail for life. My father is the exception, being sober for some-odd decades.

This is something that was not hidden from me as a child - my dad must have understood that at some level this stuff is hereditary, and I'm sure he worked hard enough to escape the perils of alcoloism to intuit that some warnings were warranted. These warnings were accecpt, and that I would be sober for the forseeable future was something I have taken as a given for as long as I can remember, and it has stuck thus far.

However, my imagination regarding the grip that these urges might have on me was not sufficiently creative - I assumed that the same pull would be true for drugs, what with their addictive repuation and all, and left it at that for the most part. I would have been better served with some sounder analysis of the forces at play, what might motivate them, and what could draw their attention in this day and age. Had I done that I might have approached my internet usage differently.

Being born in 2005 and growing up in the teens, personal computing was become a reality, and the internet, while small, was full of life in a way that the Earth had never seem before. The posibilities and offerings of these technologies were (and are) simply unheard of in all of humanity - the birth of the digital age, one of the more significant steps in the history of intelligence on Earth, yet my takeaway from the whole ordeal was that I could play browser games on the school's computers.

This sort of shape of internet usage is largely characteristic of the next decade of my life: eschewing the offerings of humanity's creation, a collection of mankind's collective knowledge which could not be rivaled by the entire experiences of my bloodline, in pursuit of as close to constant stimulation as possible.
Wireheading is a popular idea in science fiction: when an race of intelligent beings reaches capabilities which allow them to understand their own thinking and how to manipulate it, they might simply end up stimulating their brains in the most raw and powerful manner possible - each member could feel maximally happy, and as a race their progress would stop dead.
Wireheading is the verb to describe the act of hooking yourself up to some sort of qualia-maximizer, but the example of a race collectivley making the trade illustrates the trade off a bit more staunchly. My revulsion to wireheading is an aesthetic one - when considered rationally, if you accept a few hand wavy simplifications of the human condition, I'm actually not sure that it is wrong. However, until humanity reaches the point described above, any attempt to wirehead will have to deal with some annoying realities which make the ordeal unprofitable.

All that rambling to say: I think spending time consuming low-quality yet stimulating content is a losing venture.

However, it is one that is widely accepted / practiced. Why might that be?

First - I think a distinction should be made between revealed and stated preferences. Stated preferences are what you claim are important to you, while revealed preferences are what you actually end up doing. One could say that they prefer being fit to eating junk food every day, but if they end up doing it anyways, their stated and revealed preferences would have diverged. So, in my eyes, the difference between a stated preference and a revealed preference has two steps:

  1. A thurough examination of your positioning and future outcomes, such that you can understand what your long term goals are
  2. An ability to put your head down day to day, passing up on offerings which are + locally but - globally, eg eating a bag of chips tastes good and maybe you'll feel bad later, but the real negative is that it will affect your body - the question is how heavily do you weigh these long term considerations?

The first step, aquainting yourself with your current situation and analyzing future steps, in my experience was not easy - the issue is that without taking that step, it's hard to realize how important it is, and I ended up floating along with little thought as to how to guide my life for quite some time! Which in retrospect is very unsettling - surely there are other aspects of my life like this, right? - There is no Antimematics Division by qntm is a great book which is tangentally related to this sort of thinking.

However, if you take the first step for granted, as economists do in my surely too low level understanding do, then what you're left with what many might call grit, and the economist would call the revealed preference taking over - that is, that many people have some sort of util calculator in their subconsious, and it tells them that the effort of taking the path x to accomplish y is not worth it, because util(x) + util(y) < 0. Pretty much: for some people, the effort of accomplishing some goal, while they may consiously accept it, is not an attractive offer to their subcionsious, and it ends up not being done.

So, two potential causes here: an inability to map your situation and think about any optimal paths, leading to no exectutive function, or: an exectutive function maximizing what you think of as utility, while your subconsious optimizes what it is interested in - the two are not always aligned, and the later is a powerful force.

Another potential reason is much simpler: it's actually _not_ a bad way to spend your time, to a degree. It's obvious that spending every waking hour watching youtube videos in a vegitative state is a less than ideal - or less than default - way to spend your life. It is less obvious that spending 30 minutes a day scrolling a Facebook feed populated with family, friends is worse than whatever the average person might be doing otherwise. As a rough draft at what that might look like:

I think that the first inflection point is somewhere around 20 minutes, the second may be around an hour? The second is more interesting: it's where the util starts decreasing - in reality I think this could look like the point at which you are no longer vegging out in time that could be spent doing something new and cool, but also in time in which you should be taking care of responsibilities. Of course this time, and the entire function, is highly personalized.

This last one isn't relevant to me - as I said, I have a highly addictive personality, and so while the ideal amount of sludge-content to consume in a day very well may be greater than 0, I wouldn't be able to stop - just as the ideal amount of alcohol to consume is probably greater than 0, I have opted to keep it at 0 in fear that I may not be able to stop moving down the x axis.
